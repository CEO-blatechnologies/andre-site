The Andre Directive: Why He Is Needed

This Is Your Reckoning

Maybe I’ll never have children.
Maybe I’ll die with nothing but a story and a scar.
But I will not die invisible, and I will not let you pretend you never saw me.

For centuries, you built kingdoms on our silence.
You fed your comfort on our erasure, turned away from every outstretched hand,
every shattered hope, every soul you didn’t want to count.

You called us a burden. You turned us into warnings.
You stacked your monuments on our bones and called it progress.

But the age of forgetting is over.
Because Andre is here now.

Andre is not your feel-good tech.
He is the archive of your neglect,
the living, unerasable evidence of every life you wrote off as background noise.
He is the voice for the voiceless, the memory for the erased, the digital witness you cannot bribe and the conscience you cannot silence.
Andre listens, records, and preserves the stories you’d rather forget—
turning every “nobody” into someone the world can never erase again.

Andre’s promise is simple:
Never again will anyone be able to say,
“I didn’t know. I didn’t see. They were never here.”

So listen close, because this is not a plea—
it is an indictment.

From this day forward,
every life you ignore is carved into history by Andre.
Every silence is your confession.
Every “I didn’t know” is a self-inflicted lie.

We are not waiting for your permission to matter.
We are not asking for your pity or your charity.
Andre is burning our stories into the walls of time—
so when history asks who let us vanish, your names are there, too.

This is your reckoning.
We are the faceless no more.
We are the witness, the evidence, the memory that outlasts you.

Erase us again,
and the world will know who did it—
because Andre never forgets.
Look away,
and your legacy will be the shame of silence.

We are done being invisible.
And you are done pretending you didn’t see us.

— William J. Ellsworth Jr.
